That is a frustrating loop to be in—having the watch tell you there is "no internet" when you can clearly see it's connected to Wi-Fi or your phone.

This usually happens because the Google Account authorization has "stale" data between the watch and the phone, or the watch is struggling to bridge the internet through Bluetooth.

Here is a list of steps to fix it, ordered from the easiest to the most effective:

1. The "Re-Sync" (Often the culprit)
If you recently changed your Google password or updated security settings, the watch might be "logged out" in the background even if it looks fine.

On your phone, open the Watch/Wearable app.

Go to Watch Settings > Google > Accounts.

Remove your Google account from the watch, then add it back.

This forces the watch to re-verify its "handshake" with Google's servers.

2. Toggle "Cloud Sync" and Bluetooth
Sometimes the watch gets "stuck" trying to use the phone's Bluetooth internet instead of its own Wi-Fi.

On your phone: Open the Wear OS app (or the specific app for your watch, like Pixel Watch or Galaxy Wearable).

Go to Settings > System > Cloud Sync and toggle it off and then back on.

On your watch: Swipe down to settings, turn Airplane Mode ON for 10 seconds, and then turn it OFF.

3. Clear Google App Cache
A corrupted cache in the Google app itself can trigger this error.

On your watch, go to Settings > Apps > App info > System Apps.

Find Google Assistant (or just "Google").

Tap Clear Cache and Clear Data. (You may need to set up "Hey Google" again after this).

4. Check "Private DNS"
If your phone has a "Private DNS" or a VPN enabled, it can sometimes block the watch from "tunneling" through the phone's connection.

Try turning off any VPN on your phone.

On your phone, go to Settings > Network & Internet > Private DNS and set it to Automatic (or Off) to see if the watch starts working.

Quick Tip: Check if the Google Play Store on your watch can load. If the Play Store works but Assistant doesn't, it’s definitely a Google Account issue (Step 1). If the Play Store also says "no internet," it's a hardware/Bluetooth connection issue (Step 2).
